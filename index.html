Coral Reefs and Sea Grass Breds



With 17 million inhabitants and a 1,585 km-long coastline, Sri Lanka contains nearshore coral reefs of various condition over around 2 percent (up to 32 km) of the country's coastline. The majority of reefs on the continental shelf are patch reefs or fringing reefs that are located at various distances from the shore on rocky substrate.

Suitable Nesting Beaches



Female sea turtles come ashore at sea turtle nesting beaches to lay their eggs. Because they offer a secure shelter for their eggs and hatchlings, these beaches are essential to the survival of sea turtles. Other species, including shorebirds and crabs, depend on nesting beaches as vital habitats.
